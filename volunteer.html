The North Star Award given to Brad Truman

The North Star Award represents someone who truly embodies the core values, mission, and vision of this organization. The North Star is what guides your decisions and keeps you on course—much like the sailors who once used Polaris to navigate the seas. In the same way, this award honors a team member whose actions consistently guide us toward our purpose.

This is the first year of this annual award, making today especially meaningful. Brad exemplifies the diligence, compassion, and unwavering commitment required to serve the homeless in our area. Brad understands how imperative it is to show up, speak up, and fix what needs to be fixed in order to provide safety and security to the 348 people who rely on our shelters, our housing, and dining in our soup kitchen every single day. Brad, thank you for being a key member of this team. Thank you for caring (even when you don’t want people to know you do!). Thank you for going above and beyond your job description, and for choosing every day to make someone else’s life better. Your dedication steadies us, inspires us, and truly guides us.

Carolyn Neighoff, Painting a Brighter Future

Forty years ago, a compassionate vision took root in a church soup kitchen, sparked by the unwavering dedication of Carolyn Neighoff. That vision has blossomed into the Bread of Life, a statewide organization that now extends a lifeline to hundreds through daily meals, provides hundreds of stable housing units, and operates multiple shelters.

What began as a heartfelt response to immediate needs has become a powerful force for change across Maine, all thanks to Carolyn's enduring commitment. Even after four decades, Carolyn's hands are still at work, not just in guiding the organization, but often found painting, adding beauty and warmth to the spaces that embody her life's work – a beautiful metaphor for her ongoing dedication to painting a brighter future for so many.

Kindness and Love": Our Volunteers Speak

At Bread of Life, our mission is driven by compassion, and we are so grateful for the dedication of our incredible volunteers. We often hear how special the atmosphere is, and we love hearing it directly from those who give their time.

As volunteer Ben beautifully put it, Bread of Life is "full of kindness and love." This sentiment truly encapsulates the heart of what we strive to create every day – a welcoming, supportive, and compassionate environment for everyone who walks through our doors, both those we serve and those who serve alongside us.

Augusta Elks - Lodge 964

The Augusta Maine Elks Lodge has consistently demonstrated an unwavering commitment to the Maine Bread of Life, serving as a pillar of support for their vital mission. Through dedicated fundraising initiatives, the Elks Lodge has provided essential financial resources, enabling the Bread of Life to continue offering sustenance and hope to those in need within the community.

Beyond monetary contributions, members of the Augusta Elks Lodge regularly volunteer their time and effort, actively participating in the day-to-day operations and special events of the Maine Bread of Life. This continuous and multifaceted support exemplifies the Elks' enduring dedication to local charitable endeavors and their profound impact on the well-being of their fellow citizens.
